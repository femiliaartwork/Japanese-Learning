First way of using
Noun A + の + Noun B
Noun B = main item
Noun A = Added Info

Second way of using is for possessive ('s)

e.g　私の携帯　- watashi no keitai (phone = Noun B Mine = Noun A) emphasize who's phone, my phone 
　　　　幼稚園の先生 - yochien no sensei (sensei = Noun B yochien = Noun A) emphasize what kind of teacher, a kindergarden teacher
	やきゅうの選手 - yakyu no senshu (senshu = Noun B yakyu = Noun B) emphaize what kind of teacher, a baseball player

this and that in japanese
これ - this (near you) e.g. これはなんですか　（what is this?）
それ - that (near them)　e.g. それはピカチュウ一です (that is a pikachu) 
あれ - near (near nobody)e.g. あれはなんですか　(what is that ---in the distance)
どれ - which? (for 3 or more things)　e.g. どち (which?)

We can also say in conversation なんですか、それ？（although それ is at the back but there is a  か particle so people know its a question）

Specific this and that in japanese
この - this 
その - that 
あの - that (in a distance)
どの - which?

Difference between この and これ?
この requires a noun after (この + noun)
これ does not
e.g. この本／その本／あの本／どの本
Example sentences:
この本はほもしろいですか　- sono hon wa homoshiroi desu ka? (Is this book interesting?)
そのポケモンはピカチュ一ですか (Is that pokemon pikachu?)
どのポケモン - dono pokemon (which pokemon?)

Too/also in japanese
- In order to use this particle も you need to have at least two sentence first the same as english
Z も　Y　です
Z = Topic
も　= too
Y = About Z
です = It is
Example Sentence: かれはアメリカ人です。　僕もアメリカ人です　kare wa amerika jin desu. Boku mo amerika jin desu. (He is an american. I am american too)

negative statement opposite of です
ではありません　／　じゃない 
X は　Y　じゃない - (X is not y) 
X = topic
は = topic marker
Y = something about X
じゃない = is not
Example Sentence: 
バナナは赤じゃない　- banana wa aka janai (banana is not red)
今日は月曜日じゃない - kyo wa getsuyobi janai (today is not monday)

よ and　ね 
よ - "I tell you" i.e. Confident in statement, new information to listener
ね - "right?" i.e. Seeking agreement or confirmation

Sentence + よ
Sentence + ね
バナナは甘いですよ、りんごも甘いです - banana wa amai desu yo, ringo mo amai desu yo (I am telling you that banana is sweet, apple too!)
バナナは甘いですね、りんごも甘いですね banana wa amai desu ne?, ringo mo amai desu ne (Banana is sweet right?, Same for apple right?)

Particles を　で　に　へ　

を - marks directs objects　/ marks place of movement 
Direct object + を + verb
Example Sentence: 
コ一ひ一を飲みます - kohi wo nomimasu
テレビを見ます - terebi wo mimasu

で - marks where an activity takes place
location + で + verb 
Example Sentence:
家でコ一ひ一を飲みます - ie de kohi wo nomimasu (I will drink coffee at home)
バ一でテレビを見ます　- bar de terebi wo mimasu (I will watch tv at the bar)

に - Marks the time an activity takes place or Movement towards a goal
Time + に + verbs
Location + に + Movement verb (go)
Example Sentence: 
7 時に家でコ一ひいちを飲みます　- shichi ji ni ie de kohi wo nomimasu (I will drink coffee at home at 7o clock)
日曜日にバ一デでテレビを見ます - nichiyoubi ni baa de terebi wo mimasu (I will watch tv at bar on sunday)


---に します/---に する
---ni shimasu/---ni suru

Suru（する）means to do, to make, to play, to wear depending on the context. When suru（する）is preceded by the particle ni（に）, it means "decided on ---" or "make it ---."

********** Example **********

A: 私は ラーメンにする。ファブリツィオは？
Watashi wa rāmen ni suru. Fabrizio wa?
I've decided on rāmen.(= I'll have rāmen.) How about you, Fabrizio?

B: 私は サラダにします。
Watashi wa salad ni shimasu.
I've decided on salad.

A: 何にしますか。
Nani ni shimasu ka?
What have you decided on? (= What will you have?)
